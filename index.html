    Project Idea

    My project idea is an android mobile app for managing your plants, both indoor and outdoor. The app will have the functionality for the user to add their plants to the repository in the app, the app will help identify the plant type if unknown or unsure and then will have advice for the plant owner, ranging from beginner to advanced gardeners. The final product will include reminders on when to water, light assessment (via phone camera), and a social aspect where users can ask advice form other plant aficionados within the app.

    Indoor plants are already extremely popular, but in recent years the popularity has grown even further. ‘Google Trends…has shown that interest in the term “indoor plants” has increased significantly in the past 5 years.’ (Why are indoor plants so popular 2018, para 1). Experts say that indoor plants decrease stress levels and reduce the amount of toxins in the air. But we are also leading increasingly busy lives and occasionally we do forget about our plant friends, until we notice they are looking a bit sad (sometimes too late!). With all these positives an app that helps a budding green thumb not kill they are new green friends must be a good idea! With push notifications coming through to remind users when and how much to water every plant they’ve add to their ‘nursery’ within the app.

    The mobile app will be like a nursery in your pocket. In version 1 (MVP) the app will have the ability to log all plants, what plant type they are, approximate size (small, medium, large) and what room in their house the plant is located. The user will be able to add photos, so they can track their plant friend’s growth over their journey and access information regarding the plant species including soil recommendations, sunlight requirements and pet toxicity. There will also be an option to turn on push notifications to be reminded to water their plants. Version 2 will start to automate more and more, with the push notifications set automatically for every individual plant type and will also include suggested water in millilitres and if needed will recommend re potting for the plants as required. We will also include the ability to have the light in the room assessed via the phone’s camera to see if the plant is getting too much/too little sunlight. Version 3 will add in a social component, so green thumbs can connect and get advice on their plant friends, if they are just at a lost on why their plants keep dying or why they do not seem to be growing they can access social forums only available to other users and post pictures and description of their issue and get advice. This social aspect is designed to help likeminded plant parents connect. But will also expand to include more outdoor gardens including things like a veggie patch, which need much more work than most indoor plants species. For users with the veggie patch function will get seasonal advice on what seeds to plant and when to water and harvest.

    I plan to use a tool called ‘Appy Pie’ to build the MVP and make available on the app store with this tool The only hardware required will be my own computer for programming, an android device for testing and then users will need to have their own android device as well.

    I have chosen to use ‘Appy Pie so that I should be able to build the MVP with my basic knowledge of programming. Once it is time to start working on version 3, I will need to have advanced my knowledge in programming, especially in web development languages like HTML 5 and CSS. There is extra study I can do in app development as well as the skill I am learning through university.

    Once successful users will have a solution to managing their indoor and outdoor plants all managed with their phones. It does not how busy you are; you will now never forget to look after your plant friends.
